Everything about Google reviews for pubs

Digital word of mouth: Google reviews for Irish pubs

The Irish pub has always run on reputation. A good word passed from one drinker to the next used to fill the bar. Today that word of mouth lives on Google. When a visitor searches "pub" in your town, the first listings they see are the ones in the local pack with their star ratings. A pub with 4.8 stars and 200 reviews will be chosen ahead of a rival with a handful of reviews every single time.

Tourist season: when every star fills a table

From the Temple Bar crowds in Dublin to the buzz of Galway during the festivals, visitors rely almost entirely on Google to decide where to spend their evening. They don't know the local secrets, so they trust the ratings. A strong wall of positive Google reviews for your pub is your best barman during the busy season, working 24 hours a day to bring tourists straight to your door.

Own your street thanks to reviews

Pubs live and die by footfall, and footfall is hyper-local. Google ranks listings by proximity and rating, so when someone nearby searches for a pint, the highest-rated bar wins the crowd. With enough Google reviews for your pub, you climb to the top of Google Maps in your area and pull in both locals and passing trade.
